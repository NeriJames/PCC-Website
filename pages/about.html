About Us

Mission

As we advance towards this vision, we seek to live out and maintain the following principles:

  1. We are role models in the practice of our Christian faith.
  2. We discern and develop what is distinctly Catholic in our educational system.
  3. We promote the value of excellence at all levels of the institution’s operation.
  4. As a community, we adhere to social transformation through immersion and advocacy, community outreach and parish involvement.
  5. We empower our workforce by professionalizing all operations and setting up more effective systems of coordination, communication, and networking among units of the institution.

Vision

PASIG CATHOLIC COLLEGE, as part of the Immaculate Conception Parish, envisions itself as an evangelized and evangelizing community whose commitment to quality Catholic education promotes a culture of excellence and the task of social transformation.

Core Values

“Core Values are a set of beliefs that define how the organization will live what it feels are very meaningful to its growth and development. They are small sets of timeless principles that lie behind the organizational culture.” (Klatt, 2001)

  1. Christian Discipleship
    1. Piety
      1. Manifests a strong sense and experience of God’s loving presence developed through reflection and prayer (e.g. Angelus, grace before and after meals, prayer before sleeping and rising, praying the rosary, visits to the Blessed Sacrament).
      2. Invokes the guidance of the Holy Spirit before making critical decisions.
      3. Acts reverently in church and in other places of worship and prayer and cares for religious signs and symbols with utmost respect.
      4. Participates actively in liturgical activities in the spirit of adoration, thanksgiving, and Christian fellowship.
      5. Uses the name of God reverently in prayer or casual conversation; shows wholesome, clean, and refined habits of speech and action.
    2. Knowledge of the Catholic Faith
      1. Reads and reflects on Sacred Scriptures as a means of daily spiritual nourishment.
      2. Upholds what is right and just in accordance with Christian ethics. Shows understanding of and support for the social teachings of the Church.
      3. Manifests devotion to the Blessed Virgin Mary through the imitation of her virtues.
      4. Seizes opportunities for Catholic evangelization and catechesis through Bible studies, prayer meetings, retreats, recollections, catechetical instruction, etc.
    3. Faithfulness
      1. Shows a holy fear of the Lord through faithfulness to His commandments.
      2. Respects and obeys the guidance of the Church hierarchy on moral, ethical, and social issues.
      3. Binds oneself to an unqualified avowed task, responsibility, cause or relationship as shown in prompt submission of requirements, obedience to laws, ordinances, rules and regulations, etc.
      4. Remains persistent and passionate in pursuing a task or fulfilling a promise even in the face of difficulties.
      5. Protects and promotes the sacredness of prayer life.
    4. Gratitude
      1. Displays joyful confidence in God’s loving care.
      2. Celebrates the sacraments of the Church in thanksgiving for God’s graciousness.
      3. Proclaims joyfully the good that God does for and through people.
      4. Gives honest praise and appreciation for the good done by others.
      5. Reciprocates favors received without sacrificing professional and ethical concerns.
  2. Respect for Human Dignity
    1. Integrity
      1. Takes decisive action against all forms of dishonesty and unethical conduct as shown in observing intellectual honesty, returning lost objects, reporting/correcting wrongdoing, etc.
      2. Fulfills one’s obligations and promises faithfully.
      3. Admits mistakes and accepts corrections for self-improvement.
      4. Expresses oneself truthfully, positively, and unpretentiously and inspires others to do the same.
      5. Refuses consistently to compromise truth for personal gain or to use authority and relationships for exploitative and immoral pursuits.
    2. Goodness
      1. Accepts humbly, gratefully, and unpretentiously one’s own giftedness.
      2. Respects the uniqueness of each individual.
      3. Protects the good name of others by not resorting to gossiping and rumor-mongering.
      4. Exercises prudence, good judgment, and self-control in receiving and giving feedback.
      5. Acknowledges in all circumstances God’s graciousness as the wellspring of all goodness.
    3. Wellness
      1. Respects one’s body as the temple of the Holy Spirit.
      2. Avoids food, substances, and practices that are harmful to the body.
      3. Exercises regularly to keep oneself physically fit and engages in hobbies to uplift the spirit.
      4. Participates actively in light of the teachings of the Church.
      5. Shows cheerfulness, calmness, and graciousness even in stressful situations.
    4. Cleanliness
      1. Observes proper hygiene and good grooming.
      2. Keeps surroundings clean and supports practices on proper waste management.
      3. Stays away from obscene materials and similar influences that are offensive to modesty and to purity of mind and heart.
      4. Supports actively campaigns against pornography in all its forms.
      5. Assumes the honest intentions of others and treats them in a friendly, fair, and collegial manner.
  3. Preferential Option for the Poor
    1. Empathy
      1. Makes other persons feel valued and important.
      2. Demonstrates sensitivity and responsiveness to human misery through corporal works of mercy.
      3. Exercises thoughtful care, patience, and understanding in responding to the needs, feelings, and limitations of others.
      4. Shows respect and support for the least able and handicapped.
      5. Uses appropriate expressions in showing solidarity with those in distress.
    2. Justice
      1. Promotes concern and protection for women, children, and the disadvantaged against physical, verbal, and moral abuse.
      2. Acknowledges and champions the struggles of the poor, depressed, oppressed, and exploited for a more humane existence.
      3. Helps the poor become self-reliant and empowered.
      4. Collaborates actively with agencies providing educational, health care, and livelihood opportunities for the poor.
      5. Supports Church-initiated advocacy programs on current social, economic, environmental, and political concerns.
    3. Courage
      1. Takes a courageous stand and supports the position of the Catholic Church on current issues on morality and social justice.
      2. Manifests joyful and hopeful expectations of God’s unfailing providence.
      3. Takes risks in fighting the evils of apathy, complacency, and cynicism.
      4. Offers active support for the victims and survivors of violence, exploitation, and oppression.
      5. Stays kind and friendly even when confronted with rejection, hostility, or unfavorable judgment.
  4. Excellence
    1. Competence
      1. Acknowledges faith in Jesus Christ as the source of human knowledge and values.
      2. Shows love for assiduous lifelong education through reflective inquiry, rational problem solving, team learning, etc.
      3. Pursues high achievement standards in everything one does.
      4. Seeks specialization in particular fields of study while pursuing concerns for integral human formation.
      5. Reflects on links between knowledge and current social realities and takes appropriate action to support transformational change.
    2. Resourcefulness
      1. Seeks to gain and generate new knowledge from learning opportunities in the World Wide Web, community resources, etc.
      2. Treasures team learning through skillful discussions and dialogues in groups.
      3. Accepts and appreciates the help and support extended by others.
      4. Explores diverse opportunities for obtaining comprehensive information and varying perspectives on a given issue.
      5. Utilizes personal and community resources judiciously and efficiently.
    3. Discipline
      1. Works responsibly without need for close supervision.
      2. Shows consistency in performing tasks systematically and competently.
      3. Adheres to high standards of professionalism and work ethics.
      4. Shows refinement, propriety, and orderliness in dealing with superiors, peers, and others.
      5. Practices self-reliance and diligence to avoid undue dependence.
  5. Responsible Stewardship
    1. Charity
      1. Manifests humility, simplicity, and frugality in one’s lifestyle.
      2. Shares unconditionally and generously one’s talents, time, and resources with those in need for the common good.
      3. Shows patient endurance in reaching out to others towards reconciliation, justice, and peace.
      4. Demonstrates utmost respect for the beneficiaries of one’s charitable works.
      5. Exudes zeal for the good of souls.
    2. Dependability
      1. Makes oneself available for service where and when needed.
      2. Works beyond what is expected generously, freely, and voluntarily.
      3. Completes tasks and solves problems in ways that benefit others.
      4. Performs difficult tasks with joyful expectation of God’s loving providence and inspires others to do likewise.
      5. Shows initiative, perseverance, and determination in meeting responsibilities.
    3. Prudence
      1. Evaluates and reflects on any work done before taking further action.
      2. Acts according to the dictates of a clear and well-formed conscience.
      3. Respects the counsel and direction offered by proper authorities particularly on moral and ethical issues.
      4. Shows positive values and refined habits of speech and action in social interactions.
      5. Respects and protects the confidentiality of privileged communication.
    4. Love for Environment
      1. Conserves water and energy.
      2. Uses, re-uses, and recycles materials properly.
      3. Supports actively advocacy programs for the care and protection of the environment.
      4. Shows creativity and inventiveness in discovering new ways of conserving the earth’s non-renewable resources.
      5. Uses available resources judiciously, efficiently, and responsibly.
  6. Commitment to the Building of the Local Church
    1. Communion
      1. Contributes to the well-being of the family and helps create and maintain a family climate in school.
      2. Promotes harmonious relationships based on mutual trust and respect.
      3. Exercises the spirit and ways of fraternal correction.
      4. Accepts and performs committee assignments and related tasks willingly.
      5. Imposes on oneself voluntarily and willingly high achievement expectations in work and service for the good of the family, school, or community.
    2. Parish Consciousness and Involvement
      1. Joins and actively involves oneself in at least one parish organization or ministry.
      2. Participates actively in spiritual formation programs in the parish.
      3. Gives generous financial support for amelioration programs for the poor.
      4. Participates actively in the liturgical celebrations of the parish.
      5. Supports the thrust of the parish in building small Christian communities.
    3. Nationalism
      1. Appreciates the rich cultural diversities of Filipinos and promotes the best of Filipino customs and traditions.
      2. Upholds the rights, privileges, duties, and responsibilities of Filipino citizenship.
      3. Practices critical analysis and reflection to see the interrelationships of significant historical events.
      4. Works to liberate self and others from the influences of mediocrity, complacency, and cynicism.
      5. Promotes international understanding and world peace.

History

The Immaculate Conception: Pasig Catholic College’s Patron Saint

The Immaculate Conception, the Beloved Mother and Special Protector, is PCC’s patron saint. The Spanish Missionaries placed the whole country under her special protection by making her the country’s patron saint. During the 1760’s, Pasig was one of the few immediate areas that saw the presence of the Spaniards. Settlements were built around the church, a familiar structure and one of the living pillars of the Hispanic era. Soon, priests were seen outside of the pulpit, gathering people, children and old folks alike, preaching and educating them on the values and virtues of Christianity. For these reasons, Pasig Catholic College, presently a Diocesan College of Pasig, has long adopted the Immaculate Conception as its patroness. Mary’s hidden life, an eloquent testimony of deep faith, purity, courage, wisdom and obedience to the Father’s Will is what the College has always wished for her students and employees to emulate.

The De Brouwer Legacy: PCC’s Early Years

Out of the desire and determination of the Belgian CICM Missionaries to build a Catholic school for teaching children the love of God while passing on the cultural heritage and tradition of the people, Pasig Catholic College was founded by Rev. Fr. Pierre Cornelis de Brouwer, CICM in 1913. It was known as “Escuela Catolica” during the early 1900’s.

In 1916, during the time of Rev. Fr. Paul Hubaux, CICM, the American government recognized the primary school. In 1920, under the administration of Rev. Fr. Godfried Aldenhuijsen, CICM, the intermediate levels were approved for operations. As the population grew in 1931, Rev. Victor De Klerck, CICM saw the urgency of constructing what would be the school’s first building, Bahay Paaralan, composed of eight classrooms built in the Church patio. In 1939–41, the Urbano Building was constructed under Rev. Fr. Urbain (Urbano) Timmermans, CICM.

Pasig Catholic College was originally incorporated in accordance with Philippine Laws on 24 August 1915. After World War II, the Articles of Incorporation were reconstituted on 12 August 1948 and filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC). The transcript of these Amended Articles of Incorporation was issued on 03 February 1949.

Rev. Tjolle’s Arrival in 1951

The arrival of Rev. Fr. Roger Bruno Eduard Tjolle, CICM, in 1951 opened a new chapter for the school, now known as Pasig Catholic College, as secondary and tertiary levels were duly recognized. Rev. Fr. Tjolle introduced the American label of education to the Philippines through PCC.

Several infrastructure changes were seen during the administration of Rev. Fr. Josef Van den Daelen, CICM (1957–1962). In 1965, he appointed lay Filipino administrators to key school positions. Rev. Fr. Karel Ooteghem, Rev. Fr. Paul de Bevere, and Rev. Fr. Lambert Smits—all CICM priests—later became PCC school directors from the mid-60s to early 70s.

From Belgian to Filipino Directors

Founded by CICM, PCC moved towards Filipinization in 1973 with Msgr. Gaudencio B. Rosales as School Director under MAPSA administration (1973–1979), followed by Msgr. Manuel Sobreviñas (1979–1993), Msgr. Emmanuel V. Sunga (1993–1997), Msgr. Manuel G. Gabriel (1997–2003), Msgr. Gerardo O. Santos (2004–2015), Rev. Fr. Orlindo F. Ordoña (2015–2021), and Bp. Mylo Hubert C. Vergara (2021–present).

First Filipino Director: Msgr. Gaudencio B. Rosales

Before becoming an iconic figure of the “Pondo ng Pinoy” advocacy, Msgr. Rosales served as PCC School Director (1973–1976). He emphasized that PCCians, given quality Catholic education rooted in heritage and character, will achieve success and eternal life with God.

Msgr. Sobreviñas’ Charism

Msgr. Manuel Sobreviñas’ charismatic leadership strengthened PCC’s culture of service and volunteerism. Serving concurrently as MAPSA Superintendent, he helped solidify MAPSA’s institutional development.

A Wave of Changes Under Msgr. Sunga’s Leadership

Msgr. Emmanuel Sunga and Assistant Director Rev. Alfredo M. Ramos oversaw major developments, such as the five-story Administration (now Msgr. Suñga) Building, new school canteen, and the gradual introduction of co-education in the college department.

In the Fullness of Time with Msgr. Gabriel

In 1997, Msgr. Manuel G. Gabriel’s leadership ushered organizational reforms, participatory management, updated vision-mission, establishment of the Research & Development Center, and the creation of the Christian Formation Office. PCC also achieved its first PAASCU Level I and Level II accreditations during this time.

From Parochial to Diocesan: The Stewardship of Bishop San Diego

In 2003, the Diocese of Pasig was formally recognized, with Bishop Francisco C. San Diego, D.D., as its founding bishop and Chairman of the PCC Board of Trustees.

PAASCU and PACUCOA Accreditation

In 2018, the Grade School and High School received PAASCU Level III Re-Accredited Status. The College’s Education and Business Administration programs achieved Level II Accreditation, while the School of Graduate Studies earned its PACUCOA Level I Status.

PCC as a “Little Acre of Grace”: Msgr. Santos’ Legacy

Msgr. Gerardo O. Santos (2004–2015) infused dynamism, innovations, curriculum integration of values, partnerships, staff benefits, and major infrastructure including the Centennial Building. He introduced the phrase “Little Acre of Grace” and strengthened the school’s core values.

Pasig Catholic College with Bishop Vergara: A Centennial Institution

Under Bishop Mylo Hubert C. Vergara, PCC focused on formation, ecological involvement, pastoral priorities, diocesan programs, and strategic educational leadership. His pastoral visits (2013 and 2020) reinforced PCC’s mission and community spirituality.

Rev. Fr. Ordoña’s Leadership Priorities

Rev. Fr. Orlindo F. Ordoña strengthened Christian formation, K-12 transition, accreditation, Centennial Building completion, and pandemic response programs, including the Learning Continuity Program and virtual accreditation.

Reconvening of PaDSS Commissions

PaDSS Commissions conducted strategic planning in 2021–2022, focusing on performance review, strategy development, and execution.

Missio et Evangelizatio

At 109 years, PCC embraced the theme “Missio et Evangelizatio,” aligning with the Church’s 500 years of Christianity celebration, emphasizing healing, mission, and evangelization.

PCC: Scaling Up Excellence in Catholic Education

PCC remains committed to forming young people in character and competence. Under Bp. Vergara as Acting President and Fr. Daniel L. Estacio as Assistant to the President, the school continues to push excellence, curricular innovation, strategic partnerships, and strong Catholic formation.

PCC’s roots stem from the noble heritage of its CICM founders. More than a century later, its mission of evangelization, service, academic excellence, and social transformation continues—reviewing the past, facing the present, and conquering the future.
